ECAQUA BOT is an autonomous smart irrigation robot that follows a black line, checks soil conditions, and waters plants only when needed to save water.
ECAQUA BOT uses color sensors to follow a black line on the ground, allowing it to move automatically without any remote control.
The robot is programmed to stop at specific points along the line where plants are located so it can check the soil.
A soil moisture sensor is inserted into the soil using a movable arm to detect whether the soil is wet or dry.
Wet soil contains enough water molecules for nutrients to dissolve and move to plant roots, while dry soil lacks water and slows nutrient movement.
Fish tank water contains natural nutrients like nitrates from the nitrogen cycle, which help plants grow and reduce the need for chemical fertilizers.
ECAQUA BOT saves water, prevents over-watering, protects soil chemistry, and supports sustainable agriculture.
Future goals include adding pH sensors, using solar power, applying AI for better predictions, and expanding the system for real farms.
